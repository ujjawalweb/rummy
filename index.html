Rules to Play Rummy Online?

The rules of playing rummy online do not differ much from the generic rummy rules for playing the offline form of the game. At A23, we adhere to the rules that govern the general Indian rummy that has been played for decades.

13 Card Rummy:
The 13-card rummy is the traditional form of the game and involves two decks of cards, including one joker per deck. Aces are ranked the highest, and the order goes: 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9 10 Jack Queen King Ace. The Ace card can be used either as a number card (1), or as a face card when making sets, like 1, 2, 3, or Queen, King, Ace.

Scores Calculation:
The players score is the total value of all the cards and that are not a part of a valid sequence and a set. The objective is to reduce your score to 0 by forming valid sequences & sets. The cards have values as follows: Face cards (A, K, Q, J) - 10 points. The value of the rest of the card are equal to their rank.

A few Important Rules to Follow Playing Rummy on A23:
Table:
Table is where a Game of rummy is played. Typically, one Game of rummy is played on one Table.

Deck of Cards:
A complete set of cards is called a Deck of cards. A Deck of cards consists of the following cards:
  • 52 cards - 4 Suits with 13 cards each
  • 1 Wild Card joker – Also called as a Paper Joker
Joker:
All cards of the same rank and any suit as the card selected are considered jokers.

Round:
A Round is part of a Game that starts with Dealing the cards to each player and ends with a player making a successful Show or the prize being Split.

Sequence:
A sequence consists of three or more cards of the same suit in order.

Pure Sequence:
A pure sequence is a sequence without joker.

Impure Sequence:
Impure Sequences are sequences that can contain jokers.

First Life:
The first pure Sequence is called the first life.

Second Life:
The second Life can be a second pure sequence or the first impure sequence.

Sets:
Sets are a group of 3 or 4 cards with the same rank but different suits. Sets can contain jokers. Learn more

Varients of Rummy on A23

Points Rummy:
Of all the variants, this variant moves the fastest. Points Rummy brings together the thrill of sped and the skill required to play rummy. Each game lasts for a single deal. This means, players get to play more games in a shorter period! Shorter games require a higher level of skill and keep the players on the edge of their seats!

Pool Rummy:
A spin-off of the traditional 13-card Indian rummy, players participating in Pool Rummy are required to pay an entry fee which goes towards the prize pool. When someone wins a deal, they are awarded zero points and the rest accumulated points are added to their score. Once a player’s score reaches the maximum limit (101 points or 201 points), they are eliminated from the game.

Also referred to as syndicate, in this rummy format the player who scores the least is declared the winner. We have three pool formats on A23 site, “101 pool”, “201 pool” and “Best of Three”.

Deals Rummy:
One of the most popular variants of the game, Deals Rummy is played with chips that are provided to each player at the beginning of each game. Deals Rummy is super exciting as in this format 2/3 deals or rounds are played between 2 players and the player that has lesser count at the end is declared the winner. There is no option of drop or middle drop in this game, however if one player does not play his or her cards on timely basis, the final score is calculated based on best of 3 or best of 2 or best of 3 grand and best of 2 grand.

Gun Shot:
